How we choose our picks

We don’t run our own lab tests or time drawer slides with a stopwatch. Instead, we evaluate filing cabinets by gathering long-term owner feedback from verified purchasers, analyzing published reviews from reputable office-furniture sources, and tracking common failure points reported over years of use, things like sagging drawers, bent rails, and flimsy locking mechanisms. We prioritize models with all-steel or heavy‑duty construction, full‑extension slides (for accessing files in the back), and a track record of holding up to frequent opening and closing. Price is weighed against longevity: a slightly more expensive cabinet that lasts a decade is a better value than a cheap one that needs replacing in two years. Every pick here has earned consistent praise for its real‑world reliability, not just its spec sheet.
